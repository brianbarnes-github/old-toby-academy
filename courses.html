Eight courses across two terms. Begin with the Basic Term and finish with a recital. Featured course is open and ready to read; others are summarised below.

The Basic Term

Fundamentals · I — IV
I. ABC 101

ABC Notation 101

A school for the eye and the ear.

ABC notation is a lightweight, human-readable format for writing music in plain text. We learn its anatomy from the headers down — title, time signature, default note length, key — and then walk note by note: letters, octaves, accidentals, ties, chords, dynamics, rests. By the end of this course you will read an .abc file the way a scribe reads a manuscript.

II. MIDI 101

MIDI 101

The engine of the song.

What a MIDI file is, and what it isn't. Header chunks and track chunks; events both musical and meta; the difference between a track and a channel. Knowing the engine is the beginning of knowing why a song misbehaves.

III. INST 101

Instruments 101

Every voice in the choir.

A guided tour of every playable instrument: lutes & theorbos, fiddles of seven flavours, harps, bassoons, bagpipes, clarinets, flutes, horns, pibgorns — and the strange particular world of drums, with their many maps and unique nature.

IV. SONG 101

Song Making 101

From a tune in your head to a file on the page.

Building a basic song with the transcribing tool of your choice. Bringing the melody into ink. The essential workflow from blank page to playable .abc.

The Advanced Term

Mastery · V — VIII
V. ABC 201

ABC Notation 201

Fix what the tools cannot.

Editing text files in earnest. Troubleshooting a song. Customising notation precisely to your needs — corrections, find-and-replace, well-formed headers, useful comments. The text editor is your luthier's workbench.

Practical skills · two labs Coming soon
VI. MIDI 201

MIDI 201

Repair, reshape, refine.

Editing and repairing MIDI files in Reaper: deleting, splitting, duplicating, moving, quantising notes; bend-range correction; swing-time conversion; volume adjustment. Every operation a video lab.

8 video labs · 4 lab files Coming soon
VII. INST 201

Instruments 201

Magic at the edges.

Advanced techniques for every voice — overcoming the limitations of the system. Octave-spanning, four-octave instruments, sustain technique, the rotary effect, and the small combinations that elevate a song from competent to memorable.

Combos · trouble-spots · examples Coming soon
VIII. SONG 201

Song Making 201

Balance. Test. Repeat.

The Four Precepts in practice: never lose the melody; more is not better; find the balance; emphasise the hook. The fifth admonition — separate the drums — saves more songs than any other single change. Culminates in the Final Recital.
